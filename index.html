On Writing

My writing has evolved over the decades as I've explored different types of writing. I began with mini-shorts, then poetry, lyrics, a novel at thirteen, short stories in my twenties followed by screenplays, monologues for actors, and more recently, essays about social issues.

Immersing myself in fiction provided me with the escapism I desperately needed to get through the day after my mother died when I was four. Besides, my mother, an avid reader and subscriber to Reader's Digest (the fancy embossed hardcover ones), shared her love of the written word by teaching me how to read. Thus, it's not a stretch to understand that I would fall in love with reading after she was gone.

My first published book, The Emotional Abyss, a mixture of autobiography, poetry, and self-help is avaialbe to purchase at various online stores now! Check it out by navigating to the 'Writing Section'.
